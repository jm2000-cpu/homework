A brief introduction to black holes

Black hole is a kind of celestial body existing in space in modern general relativity. The gravity of the black hole is so strong that the escape speed in the event horizon is faster than the speed of light. Therefore, "a black hole is a celestial body whose curvature of space-time is so large that light cannot escape from its event horizon.".

In 1916, German astronomer Karl Schwarzenegger obtained a vacuum solution of Einstein's field equation by calculation. This solution shows that if the actual radius of a static spherically symmetric star is less than a fixed value, there will be a strange phenomenon around it, that is, there is an interface - "Horizon". Once it enters this interface, even light cannot escape. This fixed value is called Schwarzschild radius. This "incredible celestial body" is named "black hole" by American physicist John Archibald Wheeler.

Black hole cannot be observed directly, but its existence and mass can be known indirectly, and its influence on other things can be observed. X-rays and X-rays are emitted by the friction caused by the acceleration caused by the gravity of the black hole before the object is sucked in γ The "edge information" of X-ray can obtain the information of the existence of black hole. The existence of black holes can also be inferred from the indirect observation of the orbits of stars or interstellar clouds, and their positions and masses can also be obtained.

meteor

Meteor (meteor) is a kind of light track produced by the electronic transition of meteor body (usually including cosmic dust and solid block) moving in interstellar space when it is attracted by the earth's gravity and passes through the earth's atmosphere at high speed. Most meteoroids will be consumed before falling to the ground, and a small part will fall to the ground, which is called meteorite.

Causes

There are a large number of dust particles and small solid blocks, which may change their orbits and enter the earth's atmosphere when they approach the earth. These particles will compress the gas in front of them, produce high temperature and electronic transition, and show up as a light trail in the sky at night. This phenomenon is called meteor, which generally occurs in the high altitude of 80-120 km above the ground.

meteor shower

When the earth moves to the meteoroid group, a large number of meteoroids enter the earth's atmosphere. In the night sky, it seems that many meteors are emitted from a so-called radiant point in the sky. In astronomy, the name of the meteor group is usually given to the constellation where the radiation point of the meteor shower is located or the bright stars nearby. For example, the radiation point of Gemini meteor shower.

Meteorite is a kind of unburned stone, iron or stone iron mixed material which is rapidly scattered on the surface of the earth or other planets by cosmic meteor or dust fragments out of the original orbit outside the earth. Some of the larger meteoroids or asteroids in the solar system, perturbed by the gravity of the big planets, break out of their original orbit and break into the earth's atmosphere. Planets in the solar system

Eight planets in the solar system

The solar system is a celestial system centered on the sun and constrained by the sun's gravity, including the sun, planets and their satellites, dwarf planets, asteroids, comets and interplanetary matter.
According to the distance from the sun, the eight planets in the solar system are Mercury (☿) and Venus in turn（ ♀）、 Earth (⊕), Mars（ ♂）、 Jupiter (♃), Saturn (♄), Uranus (♅), Neptune (♆ or ⛢).

Production principle

Solar eclipse is in the same straight line between the sun, the moon and the earth. The moon blocks the sunlight, so that only part of the sun can be seen in some parts of the earth. And the eclipse, is in the same straight line of the earth to block the sun, resulting in a clear night sky.

Total solar eclipse

Total solar eclipse is a kind of solar eclipse, that is, in some places on the earth, the sun is completely covered by the moon astronomical phenomenon. The total solar eclipse can be divided into five stages: initial loss, food, food, light and recovery. Because the moon is smaller than the earth.

lunar eclipse

Total lunar eclipse, an astronomical term, is a kind of lunar eclipse. When the moon, the earth and the sun are completely in a straight line, the earth is in the middle, and the whole moon goes into the shadow of the earth. The surface of the moon turns dark red, forming a total lunar eclipse.
